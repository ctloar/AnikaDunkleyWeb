A few of my favorite projects

Meaningfulness Project

This Fall, I was presented with the opportunity to work on a new project with Heirloom Art. Co, a small store focused on meeting the needs of those passionate about homemaking and art. Heirloom is in the process of launching a new subscription box service and reached out to a group of ExDM students to add meaningfulness to this new product. Over the course of the project my team and I dove into understanding the needs of the potential end user and used that empathy data as a segway to additional market research. My team studied and applied an assortment of observation techniques including interviewing, collage building, secret shopper and picture studies just to name a few. In the end, my team felt well equipped to make reccomendations on how to appeal to the target market to meet their expecations and began to produce social media content for the company that aligned with our recommendations. The subscription service is set to launch in March 2023.



NAC Fall Conference

In my job as an events intern for BYU Marriott, one of the biggest and most extravagent events of the year is our fall conference for the members of the National Advisory Council. My team and I prepared meals, lectures, meetings, a service project and a tailgate to be executed over the course of three days. I had the responsibility of organizing the roles and logostics of my team as well leading the efforts on planning the keynote NAC Meeting. During the event I was involved with the set-up, take-down, and managment of 10 sub events included as part of the conference. This event allowed me to gain more in depth expereience planning and executing multifaceted events an allowed me to build relationshios with many members of the BYU Marriott community.



Ruby Jane Events

I have enjoyed the opportunity to work as an intern for Ruby Jane events, a small private events company that specializes in upscale weddings. Through this position I have been involved in the preparation and exection of 8+ weddings, each with unique requests and cirsumstances. Through this position I have learned about design techniques, wedding industry standards, attention to detail, and how to create strong professional relationships with the many entities involved in a wedding. The peak of my summer wedding planning experience was applying the skills that I learned as I planned, cooridinated and executed my own wedding this August. This experience gave me the confidence of knowing that I could be involved in the events space not just as an assistant, but as a leader.



Experiential Marketing Campaign

Winter and Spring of 2022 my experiential marketing class was assigned to design and and pitch an experiential marketing campaign for a company of our choice. My team and I were connected with Thanksgiving Point in Lehi, UT and were able to identify a specific component of their broad business that could use some additional marketing work. The outcome was a weekend long, open house style event showcasing the many facilities on the Thanksgiving Point campus that were available for event bookings. The event took an immersive approach, with each space emmulating a potential event to be held there. Guests would walk through a retirement party, a conference, a wedding, and a business meeting all in one night. The project allowed me to implement my knowledge of the Adobe Creative Cloud in a marketing context and apply my knowledge of events and marketing in a unique way.
